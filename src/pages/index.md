# Serge Roberge

Technology Executive — VP Engineering / Head of Technology Leadership

    

## I lead engineering organizations through periods where delivery expectations outpace the systems, structure, and operating models required to support them safely.

My work focuses on stabilizing and scaling revenue-critical technology platforms while enabling continuous modernization without operational disruption.

   

<section id="why-this-matters"></section>

## Context

Technology organizations rarely operate under ideal conditions.

Legacy platforms, limited capacity, regulatory pressure, and continuously rising delivery expectations create environments where tolerance for failure decreases while demand for change accelerates.

Progress typically does not stall because of technology choices alone. It stalls when execution, ownership, and decision-making drift out of alignment with organizational reality.

Modernization succeeds only when operating discipline exists first, clear ownership, repeatable delivery practices, and shared understanding of how systems, data, and decisions move through the organization.

In practice, critical platforms must evolve while remaining in service. The work is less about replacement than about building structures capable of absorbing uncertainty without destabilizing delivery.

   

<section id="how-i-work"></section>

## Approach

I establish direction and constraints, then give teams autonomy to execute within clearly understood boundaries.

Decisions are expected to be deliberate, documented, and revisited as conditions change. Priority is placed on fundamentals, ownership clarity, trusted data, repeatable processes, and a single operational source of truth, because these determine whether organizations scale sustainably.

Modernization and experimentation are necessary, but they must occur within systems capable of supporting continuous change without introducing operational instability.

Much of my work involves making consequential decisions under uncertainty, evolving live systems without interrupting business operations, and maintaining execution focus when organizational pressure increases.

I value direct communication, constructive tension in service of progress, and shared accountability for outcomes. Engineering organizations perform best when expectations are clear, standards are consistent, and teams understand the purpose behind the work.
